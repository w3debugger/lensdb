# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the
LensDB community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age,
body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics,
gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic
status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity
and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that help create a positive environment:

- Showing empathy and kindness toward other people.
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Taking responsibility, apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and
  learning from the experience.
- Focusing on what is best for the overall community.

Harassment, personal attacks, and other unprofessional or unwelcome conduct are
not tolerated. The complete list of unacceptable behavior is defined by the
Contributor Covenant, which this document adopts (see Attribution).

## Enforcement

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it privately
to the maintainer at **w3debugger@gmail.com**. All reports will be reviewed and
investigated promptly and fairly, and the privacy and security of the reporter
will be respected.

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards
and may take any action they deem appropriate, including warnings, temporary
bans, or permanent bans, in response to behavior that violates this Code of
Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces (the repository, issues,
pull requests, and discussions) and also when an individual is officially
representing the project in public spaces.
